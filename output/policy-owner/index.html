Policy owner

An individual or entity responsible for the creation, implementation, and maintenance of a security policy.

Description

In the context of network security, a policy owner is the person or group that has the authority and responsibility for a specific security policy within an organization. This role is critical as the policy owner ensures that the policy aligns with the organization’s security objectives, regulatory requirements, and industry standards. The policy owner is tasked with developing the policy framework, defining roles and responsibilities, and overseeing the policy's implementation and enforcement. They also monitor the effectiveness of the policy and make necessary updates or modifications as the threat landscape evolves or as the organization’s needs change. Effective policy ownership requires collaboration with various stakeholders, including IT teams, legal advisors, and upper management, to ensure comprehensive coverage of security issues. The role is vital for maintaining a consistent and proactive approach to security management and compliance across the organization.

Examples

  • The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) can be the policy owner of an organization’s information security policy.
  • A designated IT manager may act as the policy owner for network access control policies.

Additional Information

  • Policy owners are often involved in training staff on the policies they oversee.
  • They must regularly review and update policies to keep pace with technological advancements and emerging threats.
